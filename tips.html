Strategies for Picking a Bracket

  1. Do not pick too many upsets. While upsets happen every year, most higher seeds still advance.
  2. Look at experienced teams. Teams with veteran players and experienced coaches often perform better.
  3. Pay attention to defense. Strong defensive teams usually have success in the tournament.
  4. Study matchups. Different play styles can create advantages or disadvantages.
  5. Expect surprises. Every tournament includes shocking results and Cinderella teams.

Picking a perfect bracket is almost impossible. The odds are estimated to be 1 in 9.2 quintillion.
